Treatment

How do I treat myself?

There are many ways to treat an intracerebral hemmorrhage, some of which can be successfull. However, it is almost impossible to know you are likely to have an intracerebral hemmorhage. The only thing you can do is try to prevent it.

How do I stop it?

When an intracerebral hemmorhage occurs in the brain, the brain is in a lot of danger due to the increasing pressure. Most doctors and specialists focus on trying to lower the pressure in the brain. A ventilator is often used to hyperventilate the patient, lowering the pressure in the brain to a safer level. If needed, a surgery is performed in order to remove the clot, and to protect the brain from more bleeding. The pressure in the brain has to be lowered within hours of an intracerebral hemmorrhage, or else severe damage in the brain can be caused. Doctors also prescribe anti-seizure-medication as a preventive measure, for up to 6 months.
