Intracerebral Hemmorhage

It occurs in the brain: Which is part of the nervous system

An intracerebral hemorrhage occurs when a blood vessel in the brain bursts. This causes blood to leak all around the brain. If blood leaks into the brain rapidly, a sudden buildup of pressure is caused. This sudden buildup of pressure in the brain causes damage to the brain cells, which means that the nervous system would not function. This pressure could also cause unconciousness or death. This occurence mostly happens in tht basal ganglia, the cerebellum, the brain stem, or even the cortex. It can happen to anyone at any age. The nost common cause is high blood pressure, however causes can include blood clots, tumors, or even drug use.
